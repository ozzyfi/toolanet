The work order closes. But why did the breakdown happen, what evidence backed the fix, and will it recur?

If you can answer those three, you have memory. If not, every case starts from zero.

Same breakdown, same part, same intervention

The same breakdown recurs on the same equipment. Unplanned downtime, production loss, and the cost of repeated parts grow with every recurrence. Three bearing replacements in six months means three production line losses.

Work orders close, but the organization doesn't learn

Work orders get marked "closed" but the cause of the breakdown, the evidence behind the fix, and the result are rarely recorded. The same information gets researched from scratch in the next case.

When experienced technicians leave, their knowledge leaves too

When a technician with years on the field changes teams, the "this pump's sound tells me something" level of knowledge doesn't stay in the organization. The new technician learns the same breakdown from scratch.

Your AI investment is only as strong as your data

Disconnected and incomplete field records can't produce reliable answers with ChatGPT, Copilot, or Azure OpenAI either. ToolA makes data AI-ready — whatever model you choose to use.
